In 2.0, ``Substrate.complexes`` resolves substrate-exclusion ties under IIT 4.0 by the cascade specified in Albantakis et al. 2023 S1 Text: when multiple overlapping candidate substrates tie at maximum ``φ_s``, the cascade escalates to maximum ``Φ`` (structure-integrated information of the cause-effect structure). If ``Φ`` also ties for the overlapping group, the exclusion postulate is violated for that group and none of its candidates qualify as complexes; the search proceeds to the next-best by ``φ_s``. Disjoint substrates tied at any level are all accepted (the exclusion postulate is satisfied across non-overlapping substrates). ``Φ`` is computed lazily and only for tied overlap-cliques. Under IIT 3.0, ``Φ`` is not a separate quantity from ``φ_s``, so the search is greedy by ``φ_s`` descending.
