Added `pyphi.sweep(substrate, states=…, subsets=…, formalisms=…, compute=…)`, a batch driver that runs the same computation across the cartesian product of states, candidate subsystems, and formalisms and returns a `SweepResult` — a tidy long-format DataFrame (`.df`, indexed by the axes that vary) plus the raw result objects (`.results`, aligned to the rows). `compute` is `"sia"` (Φ), `"ces"` (the full cause-effect structure: Φ, distinction and relation counts), or a callable. Dynamically-unreachable states are uncomputable; when an axis is enumerated via `"all"` they are recorded in `.skipped` rather than aborting the sweep. When parallelism is enabled the independent cells run concurrently.
