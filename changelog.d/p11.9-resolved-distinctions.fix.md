**Make tied-state semantics for distinctions safe by construction**
(:class:`pyphi.models.distinctions.UnresolvedDistinctions` /
:class:`pyphi.models.distinctions.ResolvedDistinctions`).

Previously :class:`pyphi.models.distinctions.Distinctions` carried a
boolean ``_resolved_congruence`` flag and downstream consumers
(``pyphi.relations.relations``, ``CauseEffectStructure`` construction,
fold counting) emitted at most a ``PyPhiWarning`` when the flag was
``False``. That meant any caller obtaining distinctions from a
non-canonical path — ``System.all_distinctions``, JSON load,
hand-construction, regenerating after a config change — could silently
flow them into computations that produce phantom relations from the
arbitrary tied-state pick.

Two marker subtypes now make the resolution status part of the type:

- :class:`UnresolvedDistinctions` — what raw computation paths emit
  (:func:`pyphi.formalism.iit3.ces`,
  :func:`pyphi.formalism.queries.all_distinctions`).
- :class:`ResolvedDistinctions` — what
  :meth:`Distinctions.resolve_congruence` returns, and what
  :func:`pyphi.relations.relations` and
  :class:`pyphi.models.ces.CauseEffectStructure` accept. Passing an
  :class:`UnresolvedDistinctions` to either is a static type error.

The runtime ``PyPhiWarning`` in :func:`pyphi.relations.relations` is
deleted along with the flag — the static check fires earlier and
without ambiguity.

For degenerate substrates whose SIA is null (e.g., not strongly
connected), :func:`pyphi.formalism.iit4.phi_structure` now resolves
distinctions against ``system_intrinsic_information(system)`` instead
of leaving them unresolved. The fig5b paper-example fixture is
regenerated to reflect the resolved (1-distinction) result rather than
the previous unfiltered (2-distinction) tied-state pick.
