**Behavior change.** Replaced the cruelest-cut convention for system-level tied specified states with the paper-faithful per-state max-min cascade per Albantakis et al. 2023 S1 Text. ``Substrate.sia()`` now enumerates joint ``(cause_spec, effect_spec)`` candidates from the tied specs in ``SystemStateSpecification`` and selects the pair whose system phi ``φ_s = min(φ_c, φ_e)`` is greatest, via :func:`pyphi.resolve_ties.resolve_state_tie`. The chosen pair's state is recorded on the winner SIA's ``system_state``; the full tied set is preserved on ``system_state.cause.ties`` / ``.effect.ties`` and on the SIA's ``.ties`` (per-pair SIAs surfaced for inspection).

Substrates with no state ties are unaffected (cascade is bypassed). Substrates with state ties: the chosen state may differ from the previous cruelest-cut convention.

A new entry on the 2.0 roadmap, ``P11.95c — Substrate canonicalization for intrinsic equivalence``, captures the remaining theoretical work needed to make per-direction breakdown values invariant under substrate node-relabeling. Until then, ``test_invariants.py::TestPermutationSymmetry::test_sia_per_direction_phi_multiset_symmetric`` is xfailed.
