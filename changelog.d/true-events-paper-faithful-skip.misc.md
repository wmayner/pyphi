Symmetric ties in IIT 3.0 major-complex selection are policy, not paper-canonical. The IIT 3.0 paper (Albantakis et al. 2019, p.17) prescribes that two SIAs tied at maximum ``phi`` with non-comparable node sets are *undetermined*. PyPhi picks one deterministically via ``partition.lex_key()`` so downstream computations have a unique answer, but consumers should treat the choice as arbitrary on substrates whose symmetries produce such ties (the canonical example is the 3-node ``examples.basic_substrate()`` at ``current_state=(0, 0, 1)``, where SIAs over ``(0, 2)`` and ``(1, 2)`` both reach ``phi=1.0``).
