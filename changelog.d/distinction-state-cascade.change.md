In 2.0, ``Distinction.resolve_congruence`` applies a per-direction cascade for distinction-state ties per Albantakis et al. 2023 S1 Text. State ties within a single purview (MICEs tied at maximum ``ii``) resolve to the MICE congruent with the system's specified state in that direction. Cross-purview ties (MICEs tied at maximum ``φ_d`` across different purviews) resolve to the largest congruent purview — the default heuristic for "supports the most relations with other distinctions". The strict joint-relations-count alternative is captured under ROADMAP P11.95c as an opt-in research-mode API.
